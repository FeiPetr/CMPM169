Experiment X - Subject/Topic

Imitate

In my previous project, I had incorporated music in the background. In this new experiment, I kept that music in, then uploaded an image of my art into p5js. I decided I wanted to play around with image manipulation, and settled on messing around with pixelation. I found a pixel painter example online by happycoding.io and integrated their code into mine (making some slight changes) to make sure it would work with my image and existing code.

This code works by taking random coordinates, picking up the color there, and putting down a pixel of that color. It does this continuously. I decided I wanted to try and make something with more precise pixelation, and with user input. This way, I could also incorporate sound into the user input.

Integrate

I had some trouble getting the image to pixelate. I essentially took an array containing all the pixels in the original image. Using them, I create blocks the size of however large I set the new pixel size to be. Then I found the average color of each block and displayed it.

At this point I hadn’t figured out how to incorporate sound into it yet, so I decided to add functionality to adjust the background music. Luckily, p5.js turned out to have a custom setVolume function.

I was also surprised at how easy it was to add a slider.

Innovate

From here on, I wanted to connect the pixelation and the sound. I decided to make the slider correlate with both the sound and the pixelation.

I ran into a few issues off the bat. For one, the page kept freezing when I slid the slider. I realized this was for two reasons: the lowest setting being 0, therefore making the pixelate algorithm fail to calculate, and the pixelate function being called in draw, meaning it was constantly being callen. I set the default to 0.1 and made the pixelate only be called when the slider was modified.

The other major issue was that I realized that instead of pixelating based off the image, it would pixelate based off the canvas. This meant that with enough tries, the image would become watered down and blurred.

After a lot of troubleshooting, I was unable to fix this. My first thought was to add a creepy image when the image got blurred to a certain point, but I consistently ran into CORS restrictions when trying to load a new image. In the end, I settled for blackening the screen and adding the word “jumpscare” and playing creepy music when the image got blurred enough.

At this point I considered my project a finished product

Reflection

I think I did a good job overall in terms of effort put in, but I probably could have been more creative and experimental when it came to mixing sound and images. I spent a lot of time getting the pixelation to work and troubleshooting image uploads. It taught me a lot and I tried my best to circumvent my problems in a funny or creative way (for example, adding the jumpscare when I realized my image would continuously degrade). This also gave me an opportunity to play a different soundtrack, thereby incorporating sound even more.

I got really into working on this and was extremely determined to finish it on the same day I started. I may have done better if I took a break to work on it a day later, but I began fixating very hard. In the future, I might be better at creative and technical aspects if I took breaks and came back to the code later, especially since I had time before the due date.
